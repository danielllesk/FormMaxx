
## Biceps

### 1. Barbell Curl
**Description:** A classic biceps isolation exercise focusing on elbow flexion.  
**Instructions:**
1. Stand upright holding a barbell with palms facing forward (supinated grip).
2. Keep elbows close to your torso, and fixed.
3. Curl the bar upward toward your shoulders.
4. Focus on elbow flexion and no sway while going up.
**Target Muscles:** Biceps brachii (longhead and shorthead), brachialis  
**Plane of Movement:** Sagittal  
**Alternatives:** Dumbbell curls, EZ-bar curls, resistance band curls  
**Limiting Factors:** Grip strength, elbow tendon discomfort, poor shoulder stability, poor balance

### 2. Hammer Curl
**Description:** Curl variation emphasizing the brachialis and brachioradialis (forearem).  
**Instructions:**
1. Hold dumbbells by your sides, with ends parallel to your body.
2. Curl weights upward without rotating wrists.
3. Focus on keeping wrists strong and again no sway in your movement.
**Target Muscles:** Brachialis, forearms  
**Plane of Movement:** Sagittal  
**Alternatives:** Cross-body hammer curls, rope cable curls  
**Limiting Factors:** Grip fatigue, wrist stability, leveraging momentum (swinging)

### 3. Preacher Curl
**Description:** Isolates the biceps by fixing the upper arm, minimizing momentum and emphasizing controlled contraction.  
**Instructions:**
1. Sit at a preacher bench and place upper arms (triceps) flat against the pad.
2. Grip the barbell or dumbbells with palms facing upward.
3. Curl the weight upward until the elbows are flexed, then slowly lower under control.
4. Make sure you fully extend and are using a weight comfortable for you. Not training the lower half of this movement is what causes injuries!
**Target Muscles:** Biceps brachii isolated
**Plane of Movement:** Sagittal  
**Alternatives:** Preacher curl setup using an inclined bench  
**Limiting Factors:** Elbow strain, limited range of motion


## Triceps

### 1. JM Press (Throatcrushers)
**Description:** Hybrid pressing movement that combines elements of a close-grip bench press and skullcrusher, emphasizing elbow extension with reduced shoulder involvement.  
**Instructions:**
1. Lie on a flat bench and grip the bar slightly narrower than shoulder width.
2. Lower the bar toward the upper chest/neck by bending the elbows while keeping upper arms (triceps) relatively vertical.
3. Press the bar upward by extending the elbows.
**Target Muscles:** Triceps brachii (medial and lateral heads)  
**Plane of Movement:** Sagittal  
**Alternatives:** Close-grip bench press, skullcrushers  
**Limiting Factors:** Elbow joint stress, wrist comfort

### 2. Triceps Pushdown
**Description:** Isolation exercise using cables for constant tension.  
**Instructions:**
1. Grip cable handle with elbows tucked.
2. Extend arms downward, keeping elbows fixed.
3. Return slowly.
**Target Muscles:** Triceps brachii (longhead)
**Plane of Movement:** Sagittal  
**Alternatives:** Tricep kickbacks, single arm pushdowns, reverse pushdowns
**Limiting Factors:** Elbow tendons, grip fatigue

### 3. Overhead Triceps Extension
**Description:** Targets the medial and lateral head of the triceps.  
**Instructions:**
1. Hold barbell overhead with both hands.
2. Lower weight behind head keeping elbows relatively fixed, they are allowed to move slightly.
3. Extend arms upward.
**Target Muscles:** Triceps brachii (medial and lateral head)
**Plane of Movement:** Sagittal  
**Alternatives:** Dips, cable overhead extensions  
**Limiting Factors:** Shoulder mobility, neck positioning, setup is difficult with heavy weight


## Shoulders

### 1. Machine Shoulder Press
**Description:** Guided compound shoulder press that provides stability and consistent resistance through the pressing range of motion.  
**Instructions:**
1. Sit in the machine with the seat adjusted so handles are at shoulder height.
2. Grip the handles and press upward until arms are extended without locking elbows.
3. Lower the handles under control back to the starting position.
4. The deeper you go, the more side delt you target where the top half of the movement used mostly front delt.
**Target Muscles:** Deltoids, triceps
**Plane of Movement:** Sagittal  
**Alternatives:** Barbell overhead press, dumbbell shoulder press  
**Limiting Factors:** Machine fit, shoulder mobility, tricep fatigue

### 2. Lateral Raise
**Description:** Isolates the lateral deltoid.  
**Instructions:**
1. Hold dumbbells at sides.
2. Raise arms to shoulder height.
3. Lower slowly.
**Target Muscles:** Lateral deltoids  
**Plane of Movement:** Frontal  
**Alternatives:** Cable lateral raises, machine raises  
**Limiting Factors:** Shoulder impingement, momentum usage

### 3. Rear Delt Fly
**Description:** Targets posterior shoulders and upper back.  
**Instructions:**
1. Bend forward with dumbbells.
2. Raise arms outward.
3. Squeeze shoulder blades.
**Target Muscles:** Rear delts, rhomboids  
**Plane of Movement:** Transverse  
**Alternatives:** Face pulls, reverse pec deck  
**Limiting Factors:** Upper back strength, posture

## Chest

### 1. Bench Press
**Description:** Primary chest compound lift.  
**Instructions:**
1. Lie flat on bench.
2. Lower bar to mid-chest.
3. Press upward.
**Target Muscles:** Pectorals, triceps, shoulders  
**Plane of Movement:** Sagittal  
**Alternatives:** Dumbbell bench, push-ups  
**Limiting Factors:** Shoulder stress, wrist alignment

### 2. Incline Dumbbell Press
**Description:** Emphasizes upper chest.  
**Instructions:**
1. Set bench at incline.
2. Press dumbbells upward.
3. Lower under control.
**Target Muscles:** Upper pectorals, shoulders  
**Plane of Movement:** Sagittal  
**Alternatives:** Incline barbell press  
**Limiting Factors:** Shoulder stability

### 3. Chest Fly
**Description:** Isolation movement for chest stretch and contraction.  
**Instructions:**
1. Lower arms wide with slight bend.
2. Bring hands together above chest.
**Target Muscles:** Pectoralis major  
**Plane of Movement:** Transverse  
**Alternatives:** Cable fly, pec deck  
**Limiting Factors:** Shoulder strain, overextension

## Abdomen

### 1. Plank
**Description:** Anti-extension core exercise.  
**Instructions:**
1. Hold body straight on elbows.
2. Brace core.
**Target Muscles:** Rectus abdominis, transverse abdominis  
**Plane of Movement:** Isometric  
**Alternatives:** Dead bug, hollow hold  
**Limiting Factors:** Core endurance, lower back pain

### 2. Hanging Leg Raise
**Description:** Dynamic core flexion exercise.  
**Instructions:**
1. Hang from bar.
2. Raise legs upward.
**Target Muscles:** Lower abs, hip flexors  
**Plane of Movement:** Sagittal  
**Alternatives:** Captain’s chair raises  
**Limiting Factors:** Grip strength, hip flexor dominance

### 3. Russian Twist
**Description:** Rotational core movement.  
**Instructions:**
1. Sit with feet elevated.
2. Rotate torso side to side.
**Target Muscles:** Obliques  
**Plane of Movement:** Transverse  
**Alternatives:** Cable woodchoppers  
**Limiting Factors:** Lower back mobility
